Example 1
5 MarksIntegration by Parts
Question
Evaluate: $\displaystyle\int x^2 e^x\,dx$
1
Use Integration by Parts (ILATE): $u=x^2$ (Algebraic), $dv=e^x dx$ (Exponential)
$\int u\,dv = uv - \int v\,du$
2
First application: $u=x^2,\,v=e^x,\,du=2x\,dx$
$= x^2e^x - \int e^x\cdot 2x\,dx = x^2e^x - 2\int xe^x\,dx$
3
Apply by parts again to $\int xe^x\,dx$: $u=x,\,v=e^x$
$\int xe^x\,dx = xe^x - e^x$
4
Substitute back:
$= x^2e^x - 2(xe^x - e^x) = x^2e^x - 2xe^x + 2e^x$
‚úÖ Final Answer
$\boxed{e^x(x^2 - 2x + 2) + C}$
üî¥ CBSE Tip: Always write +C at the end of indefinite integrals. Apply ILATE ‚Äî Algebraic before Exponential. Show every step in By Parts.

$2I = \int_0^{\pi/2}\frac{\sin x + \cos x}{\sin x + \cos x}\,dx = \int_0^{\pi/2}1\,dx = \frac{\pi}{2}$
4
Divide both sides by 2:
‚úÖ Final Answer
$\boxed{I = \dfrac{\pi}{4}}$

üé≤ Chapter 13: Probability ~8 Marks in Board
Example 1
5 MarksBayes' Theorem
Question
A bag I contains 4 red and 4 black balls. Bag II contains 2 red and 6 black balls. One bag is chosen at random and a ball is drawn. Given the ball is red, find the probability it came from Bag I.
1
Define events: $E_1$ = Bag I selected, $E_2$ = Bag II selected, $A$ = Red ball drawn.
$P(E_1) = \frac{1}{2},\quad P(E_2) = \frac{1}{2}$
2
Conditional probabilities:
$P(A|E_1)=\frac{4}{8}=\frac{1}{2},\qquad P(A|E_2)=\frac{2}{8}=\frac{1}{4}$
3
Total probability P(A):
$P(A)=P(E_1)\cdot P(A|E_1)+P(E_2)\cdot P(A|E_2)=\frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{1}{2}+\frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{1}{4}=\frac{1}{4}+\frac{1}{8}=\frac{3}{8}$
4
Apply Bayes' Theorem:
$P(E_1|A)=\frac{P(E_1)\cdot P(A|E_1)}{P(A)}=\frac{\frac{1}{2}\cdot\frac{1}{2}}{\frac{3}{8}}=\frac{\frac{1}{4}}{\frac{3}{8}}=\frac{1}{4}\times\frac{8}{3}=\frac{2}{3}$
‚úÖ Final Answer
$\boxed{P(E_1|A) = \dfrac{2}{3}}$
üî¥ CBSE Tip: ALWAYS: (1) Define all events clearly, (2) Write P(E·µ¢) values, (3) Write P(A|E·µ¢) values, (4) Compute P(A) using Total Probability, THEN apply Bayes'. Show all 4 steps for full marks!

Example 1
3 MarksContinuity at a Point
Question
Find the value of $k$ so that $f(x)=\begin{cases}kx+1, & x\leq 5\\ 3x-5, & x>5\end{cases}$ is continuous at $x=5$.
1
For continuity at $x=5$: LHL = RHL = f(5)
2
LHL: $\displaystyle\lim_{x\to 5^-}f(x) = \lim_{x\to 5^-}(kx+1) = 5k+1$
3
RHL: $\displaystyle\lim_{x\to 5^+}f(x) = \lim_{x\to 5^+}(3x-5) = 15-5 = 10$
4
$f(5) = k(5)+1 = 5k+1$. For continuity: LHL = RHL
$5k+1 = 10 \implies 5k = 9 \implies k = \dfrac{9}{5}$
‚úÖ Final Answer
$\boxed{k = \dfrac{9}{5}}$
üî¥ CBSE Tip: Write "For continuity, LHL = RHL = f(c)" before solving. This line itself gets you 1 mark. Show LHL, RHL, f(c) separately ‚Äî never combine steps!
üìà Chapter 6: Application of Derivatives ~7 Marks in Board
Example 1
5 MarksOptimization Problem
Question
A rectangle is inscribed in a circle of radius $r$. Find the dimensions of the rectangle of maximum area.
1
Let the sides be $2x$ and $2y$. Since it's inscribed in circle of radius $r$:
$x^2 + y^2 = r^2 \implies y = \sqrt{r^2-x^2}$
2
Area to maximize: $A = 4xy = 4x\sqrt{r^2-x^2}$
3
Let $S = A^2 = 16x^2(r^2-x^2)$ (maximize S to maximize A).
$\frac{dS}{dx} = 16(2xr^2 - 4x^3) = 0 \implies x^2 = \frac{r^2}{2} \implies x = \frac{r}{\sqrt{2}}$
4
Then $y = \sqrt{r^2-r^2/2} = \frac{r}{\sqrt{2}}$. Verify maximum: $\frac{d^2S}{dx^2} < 0$ ‚úì
‚úÖ Final Answer
Dimensions: $\dfrac{2r}{\sqrt{2}} \times \dfrac{2r}{\sqrt{2}}$ = $r\sqrt{2} \times r\sqrt{2}$ (a square!)
üî¥ CBSE Tip: Always verify max/min using 2nd derivative test or 1st derivative test. Write "Maximum area" or "Minimum cost" in the conclusion. State units if given.
üìê Chapter 8: Application of Integrals ~5 Marks in Board
Example 1
5 MarksArea Between Curves
Question
Find the area of the region bounded by the parabola $y^2 = 4x$ and the line $x = 3$.
1
Sketch: parabola $y^2=4x$ opens right, line $x=3$ is vertical. Region is symmetric about x-axis.
2
Area $= 2\times$ Area above x-axis (by symmetry).
$A = 2\int_0^3 y\,dx = 2\int_0^3\sqrt{4x}\,dx = 2\int_0^3 2\sqrt{x}\,dx = 4\int_0^3 x^{1/2}\,dx$
3
Integrate:
$= 4\left[\frac{x^{3/2}}{3/2}\right]_0^3 = 4\cdot\frac{2}{3}\left[x^{3/2}\right]_0^3 = \frac{8}{3}\left[3^{3/2}-0\right] = \frac{8}{3}\cdot 3\sqrt{3}$
‚úÖ Final Answer
$\boxed{A = 8\sqrt{3} \text{ sq. units}}$
üî¥ CBSE Tip: Always draw a rough sketch! Use symmetry to simplify. Write unit "sq. units" in the final answer ‚Äî it's compulsory for full marks!
